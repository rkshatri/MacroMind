Eggs are a complete protein source containing all essential amino acids. 
Two scrambled eggs provide approximately 12g protein and 180 calories. 
The yolk contains choline, important for cognitive function.

Black beans are high in fiber (15g per cup) and provide slow-digesting 
complex carbohydrates that stabilize blood sugar. They also contribute 
15g protein per cup, making them valuable for vegetarian diets.

Tofu is a complete plant protein derived from soybeans. Firm tofu provides 
approximately 10g protein per 100g serving. It is low in saturated fat 
and contains all essential amino acids, making it an effective meat 
substitute.

Whey protein is a fast-absorbing complete protein ideal for post-workout 
consumption. It has the highest leucine content of any protein source, 
which directly triggers muscle protein synthesis.

Whole grain carbohydrates (tortillas, bread, pasta) provide sustained energy 
through slower digestion compared to refined carbohydrates. They also 
contribute B vitamins and fiber.

Bananas and apples provide simple carbohydrates for quick energy alongside 
fiber that moderates absorption rate. Bananas are particularly useful 
pre-workout due to their potassium content and easy digestibility.
