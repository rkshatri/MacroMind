Eating every 3-5 hours stabilizes blood sugar levels and prevents the 
hormonal hunger response associated with extended fasting periods. Ghrelin, 
the primary hunger hormone, rises significantly after 4-5 hours without food.

Skipping meals leads to elevated cortisol levels, which can promote muscle 
breakdown and fat storage. Regular meal timing helps regulate the circadian 
rhythm of digestive hormones.

Meal size should be inversely related to time of day for most people. 
Larger meals earlier in the day align with the body's insulin sensitivity, 
which is highest in the morning and decreases toward evening.

A light snack 1-2 hours before sleep (if within calorie targets) can prevent 
overnight muscle catabolism, particularly on training days. Casein protein 
or slow-digesting carbohydrates are ideal for this purpose.

Busy periods and high cognitive load increase caloric demand slightly. 
Planning meals around predictable schedule constraints prevents unplanned 
fasting periods.
