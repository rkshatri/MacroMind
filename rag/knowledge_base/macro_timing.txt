Carbohydrates should be consumed before and after physical activity. 
Pre-workout carbohydrates (1-2 hours before) provide readily available 
glucose for energy. Post-workout carbohydrates replenish muscle glycogen 
stores depleted during exercise.

Protein timing matters most around workouts. Consuming 20-40g of protein 
within 2 hours post-workout maximizes muscle protein synthesis. Whey protein 
is particularly effective post-workout due to its fast absorption rate.

Fat slows digestion and should be minimized immediately before workouts to 
avoid gastrointestinal discomfort. Fat is well-tolerated at other meal times 
and supports hormone production and fat-soluble vitamin absorption.

Distributing protein intake evenly across 3-4 meals (rather than consuming 
it all at once) maximizes muscle protein synthesis throughout the day. The 
body can only utilize approximately 30-40g of protein for muscle building 
per meal.
